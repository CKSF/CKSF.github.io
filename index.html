我从中国东海边的一座小城出发，被算法和机器学习吸引到大洋对岸。 在埃默里的实验室里第一次训练 Transformer 时，那种"数据里长出结构"的震撼，让我决定把学术研究和工程实践两条路一起走下去。

"如果一个模型只能在论文里跑，它就还没真正存在。"

本科毕业前，我作为第一作者把 SeizureFormer 送进了 PSB 2026 的口头报告。 随后我来到 UPenn，希望学会用系统工程的语言，把研究的想法真正落到毫秒级的现实里—— 从 FreeRTOS 上的 PID 控制回路，到分布式后端里的 async pipeline，再到把 LLM 集成进真实产品。

我相信最好的工程师同时是研究员，最好的研究员也懂得让代码在生产环境里活下去。 这个网站就是我记录这段旅程的地方：想法、项目、还有一些关于系统、模型和城市夜色的胡思乱想。

The Story

I grew up on the east coast of China and followed algorithms across the Pacific. The first time I watched a Transformer surface structure from noisy clinical data at Emory, I knew I wanted a career that lived at the intersection of research and real systems.

"A model that only runs on paper doesn't yet exist."

Before graduating I first-authored SeizureFormer, accepted for oral presentation at PSB 2026. Then I came to UPenn to learn the language of systems — from PID loops on FreeRTOS to async pipelines in distributed backends, and integrating LLMs into shipping products.

I believe the best engineers are researchers, and the best researchers know how to keep their code alive in production. This site is where I document that journey — ideas, projects, and a few stray thoughts about systems, models, and the color of the city at night.
