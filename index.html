我从中国东海边的宁波出发，本科在 Emory 学计算机与心理学—— 这两条看似平行的线，让我很早就习惯用两种语言看世界： 系统的语言回答"如何构建"，心理与用户的语言回答"为什么值得构建"。

"如果一个模型只能在论文里跑，它就还没真正存在。"

本科阶段我在 Melody Lab 做深度学习研究，与 Juntong Ni 共同一作提出 SeizureFormer， 在真实 RNS 数据上把 1–14 天癫痫风险预测做到 SOTA，被 PSB 2026 接收为口头报告； 同一届我还合作参与了 EpiDHGNN，用动态超图神经网络重新建模流行病传播。

随后我来到 UPenn，一边继续做 ML × 系统的研究，一边把身份逐渐往产品经理的方向靠—— 通过 Google PM 认证、在 Liba Space 做全栈实习并主导上线功能、在思政智能体项目里担任产品技术负责人。 我相信最好的 PM 是懂技术的产品人：能读得懂论文，也能写得下 PRD。

I grew up on China's east coast and followed algorithms across the Pacific. At Emory I majored in CS + Psychology — a combination that quietly shaped how I think: the engineer wants correctness, the psychologist wants to know why users behave the way they do.

"A model that only runs on paper doesn't yet exist."

In my final year at Emory I co-first-authored SeizureFormer, a Transformer for 1–14 day seizure risk forecasting, accepted for oral presentation at PSB 2026 (top 10%). I then came to UPenn CIS to learn the language of systems — real-time PID on FreeRTOS, async pipelines in distributed backends, LLMs shipped inside real products.

Along the way I earned Google's PM certificate and started leading Scrum, PRDs and A/B tests. Now I'm officially targeting Product Manager roles — a technical PM who can talk to engineers, listen to users, and ship things that survive contact with production.
